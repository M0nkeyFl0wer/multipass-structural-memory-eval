A Salmonella Kiambu outbreak traced to dry dog food manufactured by Mid America Pet Food of Mount Pleasant, Texas, prompted a cascading recall spanning September through November 2023, ultimately expanding to include all dog and cat food produced at the company's facility with best-by dates before October 31, 2024. Seven human cases were confirmed across seven U.S. states, primarily affecting young children, with one hospitalization and no deaths.

## Timeline and Scope of Recall

The recall began on September 3, 2023, when Mid America Pet Food issued a voluntary recall for one lot of Victor brand Hi-Pro Plus dry dog food (5-pound bags, lot code 1000016385, best-by date April 30, 2024) after a retail sample collected by the South Carolina State Department of Agriculture tested positive for Salmonella.

On October 30, 2023, the recall expanded to three lots of Victor Super Premium Dog Food, Select Beef Meal & Brown Rice Formula (5-pound bags lot 1000016890, best-by June 12, 2024; 15-pound bags lot 1000016891; 40-pound bags lot 1000016892), after random third-party sampling returned positive results. That announcement noted no human illness cases had yet been reported.

On November 9, 2023, the U.S. FDA and CDC jointly announced an investigation into seven confirmed human Salmonella Kiambu infections linked to pet food from Mid America Pet Food — and simultaneously issued a full-company recall covering all Mid America Pet Food brands manufactured at the Mount Pleasant facility with best-by dates before October 31, 2024. The expanded scope encompassed Victor, Wayne Feeds, Eagle Mountain Pet Food, and two Member's Mark varieties (Beef & Brown Rice Recipe and Salmon & Sweet Potato Recipe), sold nationwide in retail stores and online.

## Human Illness Data

CDC reported seven confirmed cases across seven states as of November 1, 2023. Six of the seven cases were in children one year of age or younger. Five cases reported exposure to dogs; three reported feeding Victor pet food to their pets prior to illness. Illness onset dates ranged from January 14, 2023 to August 19, 2023 — a spread indicating the contamination had been ongoing for months before the first recall notice. One person was hospitalized; no deaths were reported.

A retail sample of Victor brand Hi-Pro Plus dry dog food collected by the South Carolina State Department of Agriculture and analyzed by the South Carolina Department of Health and Environmental Control was confirmed to match the outbreak strain of Salmonella Kiambu through whole-genome sequencing, establishing the foodborne link.

## Product Disposition Guidance

FDA consumer guidance required disposal of all affected product in sealed trash bags placed in sealed trash cans to prevent wildlife access. The agency emphasized that pets infected with Salmonella may appear healthy while shedding the bacteria through feces and saliva, creating transmission risk to human household members without obvious signs of animal illness.
