## What this is and why it's here

A 2-hop path `A -[r1]-> B -[r2]-> C` *composes* iff the pair `(r1, r2)`
licenses a defined relation between A and C (Tarski relation algebra).
Most pairs compose to **BOTTOM** (nothing). This table is the decidable
lookup for the `ENTITY_TO_ENTITY` semantic edge vocabulary.

Relevance to SME: **Cat 2c (multi-hop reachability)** depends on which
2-hop paths are even *valid* to traverse. A composition table is the
formal underpinning for "is this labelled 3-hop path actually a licensed
chain, or does it compose to BOTTOM at hop 2?" Until ratified, treat it
as design input, not a scoring dependency.

**Vocabulary (user-confirmed grounded-12):** the semantic/relational
subset of the vault-rag `ENTITY_TO_ENTITY` edge types — SUPERSEDES,
SUPPORTS, CONTRADICTS, FAILED_BECAUSE, DECIDED_IN, DEPENDS_ON, REQUIRES,
DERIVES_FROM, PART_OF, IMPLEMENTS, DEPLOYED_ON, RELATES_TO. Structural
housekeeping edges (LINKS_TO, MENTIONS, TAGGED, SKOS, PROV-O) are out of
scope. Inverse names marked `*` are conceptual converses, not separate
edge types in the canonical vocab.

## Hard rule (kept from generation)

BOTTOM is the correct and most common answer. A table that composes
everything is useless. "Plausible-sounding" ≠ "logically licensed."
Every non-BOTTOM row above carries a concrete reason; everything
uncertain is in NEEDS-RULING, not buried in the table.
