## Cat 8 — Schema vs shape

The CKG schema declares **one** edge type (Dependencies column → implicit DEPENDS_ON). The graph itself exhibits a much richer relation vocabulary if we type each edge by its endpoints' TaxonomyIDs. The gap is the schema's effective under-specification.

| Domain | Declared edge types | Observed (child_tax, parent_tax) pairs | Multi-parent share | Max parents |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| calculus | 1 | **65** | 39% | 3 |
| us-geography | 1 | **62** | 76% | 3 |
| moss | 1 | **75** | 66% | 5 |
| glp1-obesity | 1 | **49** | 66% | 4 |
| theory-of-knowledge | 1 | **72** | 72% | 3 |

Reading: `moss` has **1 declared** edge type but exhibits **75 observed** (child_tax, parent_tax) pairs — the schema elides genuinely different relations under one label. For downstream LLM consumption that's a missed opportunity: a typed-edge format like `<X> --[INSTANCE_OF]--> <Y>` vs `<X> --[BUILT_FROM]--> <Y>` would carry richer signal at the same token cost as `DEPENDS_ON`.
